▶ Who am I

Hey there! I'm Alberto Fuente, a game developer located in Spain with a passion for crafting joyful interactive experiences, specialized in Unity. I mainly focus on the programming side, though I also enjoy game design and 3D art.

My journey into game development began in late 2020, while still pursuing my studies in computer engineering. I had the opportunity to spend my final year at Finland, where I immersed myself in the world of game development and interactive experiences.
Collaborating on various projects, both solo and with local students, allowed me to explore different mechanics and experiment with cutting-edge technologies such as Unity, Unreal Engine, VR, and AR.

During this time, I discovered my love for game development, which led me to undertake my final degree project about a comprehensive game development endeavor, for which I graduated with honors. Following this success, I was recruited by the University of Burgos to contribute to several virtual reality research projects,where I keep refining my skills in crafting meaningful and immersive experiences.

Eager to further enhance my expertise, I pursued a Master's degree in game development and programming over the past year. Additionally, I had the valuable opportunity to intern at Campero Games, where I gained invaluable insights into the workings of a professional team and their workflow, implementing agile methodologies.

I am now actively seeking new challenges where I can apply my knowledge and skill. I'm excited for the opportunities that lie ahead, committed to give my best in everything I do.

▶ Professional work

Researcher at XRAI LAB

I'm an enthusiastic researcher at XRAI LAB, an interdisciplinary group at the University of Burgos dedicated to embracing new technologies such as virtual, augmented, and mixed reality, along with data analytics using machine learning in order to develop simulators related to fields such as education and industry.

My primary responsibilities within the research group include the following:

  • Develop the necessary code and blueprints in UE5 to meet the design requirements of the simulators.
  • Validate the functionality of the VR simulators using Oculus headsets, ensuring seamless operation.
  • Design and craft virtual tools that streamline visualization and data management.
  • Extract and format pertinent data from the simulators for comprehensive analysis.
  • Methodically pinpoint and resolve any issues encountered within the designated projects.

Gameplay and UI programmer at Campero Games

Throughout my internship at Campero Games in 2023, I had the privilege of collaborating closely with a seasoned team of gaming industry experts. Together, we embarked on the exhilarating journey of crafting a prototype for an enchanting puzzle card game rich in storytelling that immerses players into a cozy fairytale.

As a member of the programming team, I undertook the following responsibilities:

  • Design the game's software architecture, using UML diagrams to illustrate and comprehend class relationships.
  • Develop and maintain the game´s codebase (C#), ensuring it not only meets the game's architecture but also embraces principles of clarity, flexibility, and scalability, upholding SOLID and Clean Code standards.
  • Build custom and intuitive Unity Editor Tools that allow designers to effortlessly craft and edit game levels.
  • Add gamefeel and juice to the whole game, including cards and UI animations, particles, effects, and overall visual feedback to enhance the gaming experience.
  • Proactively identify and resolve any bugs within the codebase to ensure an optimal functionality.
  • Integrate development processes into a version control system (GIT), fostering collaborative and organized project management while ensuring the integrity and stability of the codebase.
